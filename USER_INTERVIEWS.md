# User Interviews

## Interview 1 — Maya, CTO at a Series A logistics startup (45 engineers)

**Stage:** Series A, $7M raised, 60 employees total.

> "We have ChatGPT Team for everyone, Cursor Business for engineering,
> Copilot for engineering, Claude Pro for me and two PMs, and the OpenAI
> API for our backend. I don't actually know what we spend. I think it's
> around $3K? It's not the biggest line item so I haven't looked."

**Pain points:**
- No single owner. Finance pays the bills, engineering picks the tools.
- "Free trial that became annual" — three of their tools are on autopilot.
- Doesn't trust an AI recommendation about AI tools. "How would I know
  if Claude was telling me to drop Claude?"

**Product insights:**
- The trust angle is real. "Rule-based, no LLM in the audit" landed for her.
- She'd pay for the done-for-you review specifically because she doesn't want
  to be the one calling sales.
- The shareable URL matters — she said her CFO would never log into another
  tool, but would open a link.

## Interview 2 — Daniel, solo founder, design SaaS

**Stage:** Pre-seed, bootstrapped, $4K MRR, 1 employee (himself).

> "I'm on ChatGPT Plus, Cursor Pro, and the OpenAI API. Maybe $80/mo total.
> I'm not the customer for this. But you should let me run it anyway — I
> like seeing the audit even when it says I'm fine."

**Pain points:** None really. Curious, not bleeding.

**Product insights:**
- Empty-state matters. "Stack looks lean" needs to feel like a win, not a
  failed search. Updated the engine to surface a `keep` rec instead of a
  zero-state.
- Solo founders are not the buyer but are the **sharers**. He retweeted his
  audit screenshot.

## Interview 3 — Priya, COO at a 20-person AI dev tools startup

**Stage:** Seed, $2.5M raised, AI-native team.

> "Of course we overspend. We use everything. Every engineer here has
> Cursor + Copilot + Claude + ChatGPT. I've never said no to one because
> it costs $20 and the productivity excuse is bulletproof. Show me the
> consolidation math and I'll consider it."

**Pain points:**
- Tool stacking is a status thing on her team.
- The conversation isn't "should we cut Cursor" — it's "show me what we'd
  lose, in plain English, and let me decide."

**Product insights:**
- Consolidation recommendations need a "what you'd lose" line, not just a
  "what you'd save" line. **TODO week 2.**
- She'd want a per-engineer view: who is paying for what and using what.
  Out of scope for v1 — usage telemetry would require integrations.
- She'd buy the quarterly retainer specifically for the alerts: "ping me
  when ChatGPT changes pricing".
