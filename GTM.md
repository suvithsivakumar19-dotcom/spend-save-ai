# Go-to-Market

## ICP

**Primary:** Seed → Series B startups, 10–80 employees, engineering-heavy
(SaaS / dev tools / AI), spending **$800–$8,000/mo on AI tools**, with
**no dedicated FinOps person**. Decision-maker is the founder/CTO who feels
the spend but hasn't blocked an hour to audit it.

**Secondary:** Boutique consultancies and agencies (5–30 people) whose AI
costs scale per project and who pass through tools to clients.

**Disqualified:** F500 procurement teams (their cycle is too slow), pre-revenue
solo founders (no spend to audit), enterprises with FinOps tools (Vantage,
CloudZero) — different motion entirely.

## Acquisition channels (ranked by current bet)

### 1. Reddit — highest signal

Communities where the ICP already complains:

- `r/SaaS` — pricing posts get 100+ upvotes weekly
- `r/startups` — "what we cut" threads
- `r/ChatGPTCoding` and `r/cursor` — direct tool overlap discussions
- `r/ExperiencedDevs` — Copilot vs Cursor debates

**Tactic:** Don't promote the tool. Comment with specific numbers ("we audited
12 stacks last week — the median ChatGPT Team subscription had 2 seats and
should be Plus"). Link in profile, not in comments.

### 2. X / Twitter

Specific people who post about AI costs publicly:

- Founders sharing burn breakdowns
- VCs publishing "cost of intelligence" essays
- Engineers tweeting plan upgrade screenshots

**Tactic:** Reply with the audit URL of an estimated version of their stack.
Embed metadata makes the link itself a screenshot-worthy hook.

### 3. Hacker News

One shot, has to work. Title: "Show HN: We found $4M of overpaid AI
subscriptions across 200 startups". Need ~200 real audits first to back it.

### 4. Direct outreach to 50 YC W25 + S25 founders

"Took 60 seconds to audit your likely AI stack. Here's the report link.
No reply needed unless you want the full review."

### 5. Indie Hackers + Product Hunt

Lower priority — audience is overlapping with Reddit but smaller signal.

## First 100 users plan

Week 1: 10 direct DMs to YC founders. Goal: 5 audits run, 2 testimonials.
Week 2: Reddit comments — 3 per day, no link drops. Goal: 20 audits.
Week 3: Twitter reply campaign on AI-cost posts. Goal: 30 audits.
Week 4: HN Show HN with 100 audits as proof. Goal: 100+ in 24h if it hits.

If HN flops, fall back to a paid Reddit ad in r/SaaS ($200 budget, ~50 visits).

## What we are NOT doing

- SEO content marketing (slow, generic, doesn't match the "60-second audit"
  brand).
- LinkedIn (wrong persona).
- Cold email blasts (kills domain reputation, and we'll need it for Resend).
- A free trial / freemium gate (the product IS the free trial).
